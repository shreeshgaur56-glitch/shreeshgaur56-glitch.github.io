Forest Research Institute, Dehradun

A historic campus hosting rich tree diversity across avenues, plantations, and natural stands. This field guide helps you explore and understand the woody flora of the FRI campus.

Biodiversity of the FRI Campus

The ICFRE–FRI campus represents a living collection of indigenous and exotic tree species maintained for research, education, and conservation. Avenue plantations, experimental plots, arboreta and natural patches together capture a broad spectrum of forest types and functional groups.

Species documented here include timber trees, non-timber forest produce species, shade and ornamental trees, and ecologically important species that support local wildlife and ecosystem processes.

About this project

This website is a working prototype of a campus biodiversity information system. It is being refined step by step, and many species, images and location records are still being added.

You are invited to share corrections, missing species, better photographs, and any ideas that can improve the usefulness of this field guide for students, teachers and researchers.
